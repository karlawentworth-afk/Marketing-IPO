Marketing operations for businesses that need it to work.

clevermx is a marketing operations practice. I help marketing teams get their operations right, particularly when the stakes are high and things need to actually function.

What I do

I work on marketing operations. That means the infrastructure, data, processes, and systems that sit underneath a marketing function and determine whether it runs well or badly. Most of the time, it runs badly. Not because people are incompetent, but because nobody has made it a priority.

Right now, my focus is IPO readiness. When a business is preparing to list, marketing suddenly comes under a level of scrutiny it has never faced before. The data needs to be clean. The reporting needs to be credible. The narrative needs to hold together. Sales and marketing need to be visibly aligned. The team needs to be structured for what comes next, not what got you here. I help marketing functions get to that standard.

Outside of that specialism, I work across the full scope of marketing operations: tech stack, data architecture, process design, reporting, and team structure. The common thread is making marketing functions work properly.

How I work

I sit inside the team. I am not producing a report from the outside, or presenting recommendations from a distance. I work alongside the people who actually have to run marketing operations, and I help them fix things while the business keeps moving.

Engagements are typically short. A few months, not a few years. The goal is to get things working, hand them over properly, and leave. I am not trying to build a dependency.

This is a small practice, deliberately. One consultant, not a team of twelve. That means you get the person you spoke to, not a junior associate with a branded slide template. It also means I only take on work where I can genuinely be useful.

About clevermx

clevermx was founded by someone who spent years doing this work inside businesses, not advising on it from outside. That background shapes everything about how the practice operates: a bias towards practical work over strategic theatre, and a strong preference for things that actually get done.

The name is short for clever marketing operations. The "mx" is the point.
