D Workstream D · Commercial readiness · Tier two

Sales and marketing alignment.

Shared definitions, joint pipeline, documented handoffs. The structural work that turns marketing from a sales support function into a co-owner of growth. In a sales-led business this is the hardest of the five, and the most visible when it is missing.

What it fixes Two functions, two sets of numbers
Why it matters Pipeline is a disclosed story
The real test Do sales and marketing agree on one number
What good looks like

Two functions, one number.

The gold standard is not warmth between teams. It is structural: shared definitions the system enforces, one pipeline both functions trust, and handoffs that work on a bad week as well as a good one.

One pipeline

One definition

Sales and marketing read the same number off the same system and mean the same thing by it. The arguments about whose number is right simply stop.

Written handoffs

Not folklore

What counts as a lead, when it passes, and what happens when it bounces back, all defined in the CRM rather than carried in someone’s head.

Shared accountability

For revenue, not activity

Marketing is measured on contribution to closed-won, not on activity nobody downstream can feel. The two functions own the number together.

In a sales-led company, alignment is where marketing earns its seat or confirms it never had one.

What has to be true

Four conditions alignment has to meet.

Everyone claims their teams are aligned. Far fewer can prove it. These are the conditions that turn a claim into something diligence can verify.

01

Shared definitions in the CRM

Not aligned definitions, shared ones. Lead, MQL, SQL and opportunity mean one thing, and the system enforces it rather than relying on goodwill.

02

One pipeline number

Sales and marketing stop bringing different totals to the same meeting. There is one number, from one source, that both functions stand behind.

03

Documented service-level agreements

Handoffs, response times and what happens when they slip, all in writing and signed by both leaders, not assumed and resented.

04

Joint accountability

Marketing carries a number the sales leader recognises, and the two functions are measured together rather than pointing at each other.

What has to exist

What proves it is structural.

Alignment that lives in a quarterly offsite does not survive diligence. These are the artefacts that show it is built into how the two functions actually operate.

01

Shared definitions document

Lead, MQL, SQL, opportunity and pipeline defined once, agreed by both functions and enforced in the CRM.

02

Pipeline reconciliation

The sales view and the marketing view tied to a single source, so one number survives contact with both teams.

03

Service-level agreements

Handoff rules, response times and escalation, signed by both the sales and marketing leaders.

04

Joint pipeline dashboard

One view both functions trust and actually use, rather than two dashboards quietly disagreeing.

05

Lead-to-revenue process map

The full journey from first touch to closed-won, with a named owner at every stage.

06

Alignment cadence record

The standing meeting and the decisions it makes, the routine that keeps alignment true as the business changes.

Evidence quantum

Alignment is proven in the same number twice.

The test of alignment is brutally simple: ask both functions the same question and see if you get the same answer. Every claim here has to survive that.

What marketing says “Sales and marketing are aligned.”
What has to back it A shared definitions document enforced in the CRM, not a workshop everyone nodded through and has since quietly forgotten.
“We have one pipeline number.”
What has to back it The same total in both functions’ reporting, reconciled to one source, rather than two confident numbers that happen to differ.
“Handoffs work.”
What has to back it Written SLAs with response times that are actually met, and a log of what happens when they are not, instead of mutual blame.
“Marketing contributes to revenue.”
What has to back it Marketing’s number tied to closed-won and agreed by the sales leader, not a contribution figure marketing asserts on its own.
