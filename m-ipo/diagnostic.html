How the ten days run

Seven threads, one map.

The ten days pull on seven threads. Access comes first, because if marketing can't reach the data, that finding reshapes everything else. Dependencies and politics run underneath all of it.

1

Access and data reality · the gate

Map the CRM and GTM data with RevOps. Is marketing's CRM the system of record, or a separate database? Where do pipeline and revenue live, and can marketing reach them? If not, that is the headline finding.

2-3

The numbers today

Get the current reporting and work out what is real. Any CAC that reconciles to finance? Any attribution at all? Trace real closed deals backwards to see how much history is reconstructable.

4

Martech and AI estate

Inventory every tool: purpose, owner, cost, integrations. Find the duplication and the sprawl. Check AI governance, and whether the marketing data is held lawfully.

5

Team and capability

Who actually does what, by behaviour not job title. Does a marketing-ops or measurement layer exist at all? Where does key-person risk sit? Size the gap: build, hire, or bring in.

6-7

Story and public surface

One story or five? Pressure-test the equity story against what the data can back. Audit public claims for the unsupportable ones. Check customer evidence and permissions.

8

Controlled comms

How public communication works today, who signs off, who speaks. How marketing and comms join up. The distance to listing-grade control: financial promotion, quiet period, MAR.

·

Dependencies and politics · throughout

Who owns each blocker, where the turf lines are, who is an ally and who is a threat, and what needs a leadership mandate rather than marketing just getting on with it.

What the ten days delivers

Three things you can act on.

Not a verbal readout. Three concrete deliverables that tell everyone what needs doing, what it costs, and in what order.

01

Findings pack

The honest current state across all seven threads, in plain language, with the reds and the clock-running items flagged.

02

Gap analysis

Where marketing is today versus what a listing actually requires of it, area by area.

03

Costed, sequenced roadmap

What to do, in what order, what needs leadership, what needs hiring, and what marketing can start now.

Scope, drawn on purpose

Provable, not perfect.

The diagnostic scopes marketing's readiness and nothing else. The boundary is drawn out loud so the work stays contained.

In scope: marketing's provable growth engine, the story and its evidence, controlled communications, and the systems and data behind them. Not marketing's to fix: profitability, the funding model, RevOps' own systems, the comms team's remit, and wider business risk.

The clock is the only thing money can't buy back. With a multi-month sales cycle and a listing window a couple of years out, tracking has to start capturing early, or the data won't have enough clean history to be credible. That is why the diagnostic starts with access, and why it starts now.
