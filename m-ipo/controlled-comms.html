Controlled comms.

Every public word provable and signed off. Financial promotion within the rules, publicity guidelines, quiet-period discipline, and disclosure handled properly once listed. The part that keeps marketing from becoming the thing that sinks the float.

What good looks like

Comms that survive being checked.

The gold standard is a function where nothing goes out without a clear approval line, everything that could be a financial promotion is handled properly, and the company is ready to manage price-sensitive information the day it lists.

Publicity guidelines

Written rules, not assumptions

Clear, documented rules on what marketing can say, when it can say it, and who approves it. Not a verbal understanding that falls apart under pressure.

Financial promotion compliance

Every piece traced to its approver

Anything that could be an inducement to invest goes through an FCA-authorised approver or is clearly exempt under FSMA s21. No grey areas, no guesswork.

Disclosure readiness

A process, not a scramble

A working process for handling price-sensitive information once listed, built on the Market Abuse Regulation. Ready before the first day of trading, not after.

Financial promotion failures have delayed and derailed IPOs. The rules are not optional, and the consequences are not theoretical.

What has to be true

Four conditions for controlled comms.

These are not aspirations. They are the conditions that have to hold before the prospectus is filed, and they have to keep holding once the company is listed.

01

Publicity guidelines exist and are followed

Written rules on what can be said publicly, by whom, and through what channels. In place and followed before the prospectus is filed, not drafted after the fact.

02

Every piece of marketing has an approval line

Every outbound piece has a clear approver. Anything that could count as a financial promotion has FCA-authorised sign-off or a documented exemption.

03

Website and channels are scrubbed and controlled

The website and social channels are audited, cleaned and made consistent with what is filed. Nothing contradicts the prospectus, nothing overpromises.

04

Spokespeople are briefed and controlled

One clear line on who speaks to whom and what they say. Briefing notes, approved lines, and no improvised commentary to the press or on social media.

What has to exist

The proof that comms are under control.

For each condition, something has to exist that proves it. These are the artefacts, the evidence that backs them, and who owns each one.

01

Publicity guidelines document

Dated, signed and distributed to everyone who creates or approves outbound content. The single reference for what can be said and when.

02

Approval log

Every outbound piece traced to its approver. Financial promotions traced to their FCA-authorised sign-off or documented exemption. No gaps.

03

Website and channel audit

A dated record of the scrub: what was found, what was changed, what was removed. Proof the public-facing estate is consistent with the filed documents.

04

Spokesperson protocol

Briefing notes, approved lines, and a clear register of who is authorised to speak externally. Backed by evidence that the briefings happened.

Financial promotion compliance is not a box to tick at the end. It is a discipline that has to be in place before the prospectus process starts, and it has to hold through the quiet period and beyond.

The companies that get this right do the work early. Publicity guidelines, approval lines, channel audits, spokesperson control. None of it is complicated, but all of it has to be done properly, and all of it has to be provable.
